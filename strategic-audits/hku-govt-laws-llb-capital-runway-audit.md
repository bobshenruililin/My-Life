## Executive verdict

The GPT sample has the right **instinct**—do not confuse prestige with wealth creation—but several of its **factual assumptions are too optimistic**, and several of its **tactics are operationally weak**.

**Core recommendation (12–18 months):**

> Run a **real-options dual track**, not a premature identity commitment as “lawyer,” “quant,” or “VC.”

1. **Preserve the elite Hong Kong legal pathway** by becoming excellent at *law-specific* assessment—not by coasting on the 4.09 Global Health GPA.
2. **Falsify the quant-trading hypothesis before PCLL**, because a later “law → statistical quant research” exit is unreliable.
3. Build **one commercial wedge**: regulated technology and capital—especially healthcare/AI × M&A, data/IP, financial regulation, or private funds.
4. Treat VC as a **later ownership route**, not the fastest early-cash route.
5. Track capital in five forms: **skills, credibility, relationships, liquidity, and discretionary attention**.

HKU’s double degree is officially a **5-year / 10-semester** programme (code 6810, ~40 first-year places). Year 1 law includes Contract I/II, Criminal Law I/II, Legal System of the HKSAR, and Legal Research & Writing. The fifth year is labelled self-financed LLB on HKU Law’s tuition pages. Your transfer-credit map must be obtained in writing before any recruiting calendar is trustworthy.

# TASK A — De-biasing the Law Path

## A1. Three brutal self-inquiry questions (stronger than GPT’s)

### Q1 — Applause-Removal Test

> If nobody could know I was a lawyer, and legal pay equalled an ordinary professional salary, would I still spend years doing close textual analysis, repeated drafting, citation checking, due diligence, and client-service work?

**Run this empirically (20 hours):**

1. Compress a 40-page judgment into a one-page case note.
2. Mark up a public contract clause three times under criticism.
3. Build a due-diligence checklist from a public company announcement.
4. Ask: Did precision energise me? Did I enjoy the tenth small problem? Did I resent hierarchy or appreciate apprenticeship?

**Kill criterion:** likes legal *ideas*, dislikes legal *execution* → consider policy, investing, research, or entrepreneurship—not practice.

### Q2 — Investment-Objective Contradiction

> If my employer restricts personal trading, places securities on restricted lists, requires pre-clearance, and consumes my highest-energy hours, is elite law actually a good operating platform for an “aggressive investment strategy”?

Ask:

- Accumulate capital, or personally generate alpha?
- Would a passive core portfolio satisfy me during accumulation?
- Prefer HK$5M with no research time, or HK$2M with genuine investing skill and attention?
- Still choose law if active strategies must be substantially suspended for 5–8 years?

### Q3 — Chosen-Pain Test

| Route | Unpleasant core |
|---|---|
| Elite law | Service hierarchy, extreme accuracy, uncertain hours, low early ownership |
| Quant | Mathematical selection, rapid falsification, performance volatility |
| VC | Sourcing, social judgment, ambiguous feedback, illiquid pay |

> Which pain would I voluntarily become unusually good at for ten years?

Better than “which sounds exciting”: Would I rather be a **top-20% lawyer**, a **median quant trader**, or a **median VC associate**?

### Correct cash-velocity equation

Do **not** compare headline salaries. Model:

\[
\text{Expected liquid capital}_T =
\sum_{t=1}^{T}
P(\text{still in route at } t)
\times
[\text{guaranteed cash}_t + \text{conservative bonus}_t - \text{tax} - \text{living}]
-
\text{education \& switching costs}
\]

Treat carry separately:

\[
\text{Discounted carry} =
\text{stated allocation}
\times P(\text{vesting})
\times P(\text{fund clears hurdle})
\times P(\text{still employed})
\times \text{time discount}
\]

**Unless carry is written, understandable, and plausibly realisable → put zero in the base case.**

# TASK B — Radical Ultralearning for Law (Tech-Mind Blueprint)

## B1. Governing principle

Advantage ≠ reading faster. Advantage = turning authority into a system that supports:

1. rapid issue identification  
2. precise retrieval of authority  
3. analogy and distinction  
4. counterargument  
5. application under time pressure  

Evidence base: retrieval practice (Roediger & Karpicke), spaced practice (Cepeda; Dunlosky et al. 2013), interleaving (Rohrer), dual coding (Paivio/Mayer). Law-specific: Cooper & Gurung (2018)—self-testing and application correlate positively with law GPA; rote briefing correlates negatively.

## B2. Source hierarchy

| Tier | Sources | Use |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Authority | Assigned judgments/statutes; [Hong Kong e-Legislation](https://www.elegislation.gov.hk/); [Judiciary Legal Reference System](https://legalref.judiciary.hk/lrs/common/ju/judgment.jsp); [HKLII](https://www.hklii.hk/) | Propositions of law |
| 2 — Verification | Westlaw Asia / Lexis+ HK (via HKU); citators; textbooks; Halsbury’s HK | Followed / limited / superseded |
| 3 — Commercial context | Firm client updates; SFC/HKMA/HKEX; Conventus Law; AIIFL/LawTech events | Why doctrine matters commercially |
| 4 — Structural previews | Oxbridge Notes etc. | Outline architecture only—**not HK authority** |
| 5 — Video | HKU Law Video Archive; Oxford/Cambridge faculty; UK Supreme Court channel | Orientation—never cite as law |

**GPT correction:** Conventus Law and Oxbridge Notes are Tier 3–4. HKLII is useful free discovery, not a magical API. Prefer HKeL + Judiciary LRS for official text.


## B5. Weekly operating cycle

**Before class (30–40 min):** syllabus outcomes → legal question → statute first → judgment posture/conclusion → predict reasoning → 3 questions.

**Within 24h after class (45 min):** complete case objects → update doctrine graph → 3–5 retrieval prompts → one counterexample → one unresolved point for office hours.

**Weekly synthesis (90 min/subject):** reconstruct topic map from memory → compare → one short hypothetical → update weak nodes only.

**Fortnightly:** timed answer from Week 2 onward. Error taxonomy: missed issue / false rule / wrong authority / weak application / ignored counterargument / poor prioritisation / remedy omitted / time failure.

## B6. Better Anki architecture

Deck mix target: **15%** definitions · **25%** case–proposition · **25%** distinctions · **35%** application mini-problems.

**Bad:** “What happened in Case X?”  
**Good:** “Supplier uses mandatory language but breach consequences are minor. What classification questions arise?” / “What factual difference distinguishes Authority A from B?” / “Why is proposition P arguably obiter?”

## B7. Feynman method corrected for law

1. Plain explanation to a non-lawyer  
2. Legal reconstruction (technical language + conditions + authority)  
3. Counterexample where simple explanation fails  
4. Adversarial defence against competing authority  

If you cannot complete stage 3, you do not know the rule’s boundary.

## B8. LLM as hostile tutor (prompts)

**Case extraction:**

```text
Use only the attached judgment.
1. Procedural posture
2. Precise issue decided
3. Separate holding / obiter / policy
4. Every proposition → paragraph number
5. Three facts essential to the result
6. Three boundary hypotheticals
7. What remains ambiguous
Do not add any law not in the source.
```

**Answer critique:**

```text
Act as a strict common-law examiner.
First diagnose without rewriting: missed issues; inaccurate propositions;
unsupported authorities; weak analogies; ignored counterarguments;
remedy/procedure errors; poor time allocation.
Then rank the three changes most likely to improve the mark.
```

Safeguards: verify every proposition; never upload confidential material; follow course AI policy; keep a model-error log.

### Bayesian model — evidential uncertainty ONLY

GPT’s formula:

\[
P(\text{liability}) = P(\text{duty}) \times P(\text{breach}) \times P(\text{causation}) \times (1 - P(\text{defence}))
\]

is **doctrinally unsound**. Elements are not independent; duty is often normative; courts do not multiply element probabilities into expected liability (*Re B (Children)* balance-of-probabilities logic).

**Correct use:** contested factual inference, e.g. whether special loss was within contemplation.

\[
\text{Posterior odds}(H \mid E) = \text{Prior odds}(H) \times \text{evidential weight of } E
\]

Evidence E1–E4 (email / urgency language / industry custom / silence) → which is probative, dependent, or explained by an alternative hypothesis?

**Division of labour:** doctrine → decision tree · evidence → Bayesian/causal · remedy → consequence map · exam writing → argument + counterargument.

# TASK D — Whisper-Network Realities (evidence-weighted)

1. **Old GPA becomes secondary fast.** PCLL and firms will judge Law marks. Treat first marked Law assignments as calibration experiments.

2. **HK$100k trainee story is usually a category error.** Separate: HK trainees · HK-qualified associates · US-qualified associates · US JD summers · NY market-scale associates.

12. **Multidisciplinarity attracts interest *and* suspicion.** Defence = sustained output on one thesis, not another award.

## Playbook 4 — Outreach CRM (simple)

Rules: ≤2 highly researched professional emails/week · 2 substantive conversations/month · follow with artifact, not “great meeting you” · make one introduction *for someone else* monthly.

## Playbook 5 — Capital architecture (framework, not advice)

Maintain five ledgers:

1. **Credential reserve** — fifth-year tuition, PCLL, IELTS, relocation, professional setup.  
2. **Survival reserve** — failed conversion, delayed qualification, health/family shocks.  
3. **Mobility fund** — accept lower-paid high-upside role; move to SG/LDN/NY; fund technical course.  
4. **Core compounding portfolio** — does not require your daily alpha.  
5. **Asymmetric research account** — capped; explicit thesis; max loss; no MNPI; no career-destroying leverage.

Never count in base case: discretionary bonus, unsigned carry, unvested equity, expected promotion, uncommitted family support, future alpha.

Measure monthly:

\[
\text{Runway years} = \frac{\text{liquid unrestricted assets}}{\text{annual baseline spending}}
\]

Also: after-tax savings rate · fixed costs / guaranteed cash · unvested % of NW · employer–portfolio correlation · mobility months · discretionary cognitive hours · sleep.

# VII. Final Strategic Recommendation

Become:

> **A commercially rigorous, computationally literate specialist in how regulated technology is financed, governed, and transacted.**

- Elite Hong Kong law = **high-probability floor**.  
- Quant trading = **high-upside falsifiable option** (test before PCLL).  
- VC = **later ownership pathway**, unless an exceptional role gives real investment exposure and transparent economics.

Do not:

- purchase prestige with five years without testing the work;  
- count carry before it becomes cash;  
- count old grades as future Law performance;  
- let broad intelligence substitute for professional depth;  
- design an investment strategy your employer will prohibit.

**Decisive milestone:**

> The first time a partner, professor, founder, or investor says:  
> “This person understood the technical system, found the legal or commercial issue, and produced something I could actually use.”
