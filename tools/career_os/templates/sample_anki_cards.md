Q: A charterparty seaworthiness clause is breached causing serious delay, but substantial time remains on a two-year charter. Is termination justified?
A: Likely innominate/intermediate term analysis. Termination depends on whether consequences deprive the innocent party of substantially the whole contractual benefit. On Hong Kong Fir facts, damages not termination. Authority: Hong Kong Fir Shipping [1962] 2 QB 26.

Q: What factual change would make Hong Kong Fir more likely to justify termination?
A: If delay/defects defeated substantially the whole commercial purpose — e.g. most of the charter period lost, remaining performance useless, or adventure depended on a narrow time window.

Q: Why is P(duty)×P(breach)×P(causation) a bad model for liability in exams?
A: Duty is often normative not probabilistic; elements are dependent; civil proof uses balance of probabilities per fact/issue, not multiplicative expected liability. Use decision trees for doctrine and Bayes only for contested factual inferences.

Q: Name Tier-1 Hong Kong legal sources for statutory text and judgments.
A: Hong Kong e-Legislation (statutes); Judiciary Legal Reference System (judgments); assigned course materials. HKLII is useful free discovery but verify against official sources.

Q: What is HKU PCLL's primary academic metric?
A: All Law Average — average mark of all law subjects in the qualifying law degree — not overall GPA or degree class at face value.

Q: If your ultimate goal is aggressive personal investing, what compliance question must you ask every employer?
A: Personal account dealing: pre-clearance, restricted lists, blackouts, holding periods, IP ownership, outside business restrictions — before accepting.
